Marcus Aurelius

The last of the Five Good Emperors

A brief timeline of the life of Marcus Aurelius:

  • 121 - Born in Rome, Italy.

  • 131 - Begins to take an interest in philosophy, actively studying and taking on the habits of a philosopher.

  • 133 - A new set of tutors, including the greatly influential Alexander of Cotiaeum, take over Marcus' education. His teachings are often visible in Marcus' Meditations.

  • 138 - Adopted by Aurelius Antoninus.

  • 140 - Made consul for the first time.

  • 145 - Marries Faustina.

  • 161 - Aurelius Antoninus' death allows Marcus to rise to power and become Emperor of Rome, along with his brother and co-ruler, Lucius Aurelius Verus Augustus.

  • 162 - War with the Parthian Empire erupts, lasting through much of the 160s. The war effort was mostly oversaw by his brother Verus, much of their success being accredited to the generals working under him, especially Avidius Cassius.

  • 166 - As the end of the Parthian war approaches, conflict with the German tribes begin.

  • 169 - Verus dies, forcing Marcus to fight on alone against the German tribes.

  • 175 - Avidius Cassius betrays Marcus after hearing a rumor about his illness, and claims the title of emperor for himself. Marcus heads east to deal with Cassius himself, but Cassius was murdered by his own soldiers before he could arrive.

  • 177 - Appoints his son, Commodus, as co-ruler.

  • 180 - Dies in Vindobona (modern Vienna).

“Do not think that what is hard for you to master is humanly impossible; but if a thing is humanly possible, consider it to be within your reach.”

—Marcus Aurelius

Marcus is better known for his philosophical nature than the wars he participated in, acquiring the reputation of a "philosopher king." To find out more about Marcus Aurelius, you can visit his Wikipedia.
